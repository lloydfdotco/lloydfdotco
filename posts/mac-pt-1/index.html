How I built my own Mac with parts from Lazada and 30k Pesos (pt.1)

Personal and work related notes, thoughts, and ideas
August 31, 2020 · 2 min · 372 words · Lloyd | 

About a year ago I had been reading about Hackintosh (hacked together apple compatible hardware with the Mac OS) from Tonymacx86.com. I was particularly interested in a Customac Mini because 1. It’s the cheapest I can afford, 2. it’s perfect for what I need (some editing, web browsing), and 3. It’s in a small form factor (but it does sacrifice some performance with the absence of a GPU). Here are the parts that I ordered, all of which are available in Lazada PH except for the Motherboard which I ordered in a store (these are early 2019 prices and some of it may have been delisted from Lazada already).

Great, so far under budget! But since I didn’t read the review for the motherboard, I had the unfortunate luck to discover that the built-in wifi card is exclusive for Windows (something about CNVi) and will not work with the Hackintosh I’m building. Luckily there was a work around, I just had to buy some more parts on Lazada.
