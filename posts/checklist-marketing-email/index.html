Checklist: Email Marketing

Personal and work related notes, thoughts, and ideas
September 11, 2020 · 3 min · 448 words · Lloyd | 

I’ve had my fair share of blunders which made me more careful each time, so I made a checklist of the things to check before sending that email:

Subject line and pre-header#

  • It avoids any spam trigger words.
  • Short enough to catch attention but still make sense.
  • It fits the mailbox without getting cut off (Usually around 70 characters).
  • The preheader make a connection between the subject line and the body.

Body#

  • Readable on desktop and mobile.
  • Concise and leads to a call to action when needed.
  • Easy to understand.
  • Relevant to the reader.

Text#

  • Free from spelling, grammatical, and typo mistakes.
  • The dates are all correct.

Images#

  • Hosted online and loads properly.
  • Small enough to load on a 3g network.
  • Has an alt text just in case it doesn’t load.
  • Fits the width of the email or screen.

Smart content / Personalization#

  • Displays the right data (name, card number, balance, etc) to the right person.

Layout#

  • Mobile friendly.
  • Not overly complicated.

Links#

  • There are no broken links.
  • Buttons display properly.
  • The link to the web version is working properly.
  • The landing page is working.
  • UTM codes are used when needed.

Footer#

  • There is a way to unsubscribe / manage subscription.
  • There is a privacy policy.
  • The company’s address is present.

Subscribers#

  • Check that you have the right segment.
  • Check that the unsubscribed will not receive the email.

Sender name#

  • Using the right sender name and email

Now that you checked your email, have someone else check it. Send a test email to your colleagues and have them check the email just to make sure you’ve not missed anything.

Things don’t always go as planned#

Even after triple checking, you accidentally sent the wrong email version. Before you pack your bags and tender your resignation, there are ways to turn it around.

Missing/wrong links and images#

Sometimes your email service provider (ESP) can still re-link your broken links. These providers masks the links you provide upon sending your email, so there is an opportunity for you to re-link the images on the back end.

Typographical errors and other minor mistakes#

If it’s just a minor mistake which will not affect your customer’s experience with your brand, maybe you can just let it slide this time.
