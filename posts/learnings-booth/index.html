What I learned designing booth spaces and event stages

Personal and work related notes, thoughts, and ideas
September 3, 2020 · 2 min · 297 words · Lloyd | 

I frequented tutorial sites and learned Adobe Creative suite on my own during high school, and I got a job as a graphic designer at an events agency soon after college. A big part of the events pitch that we did include booth designs as part of a convention or stage designs as part of an event. I had to teach myself 3d using Sketchup and had to talk to the fabricator to make sure that what I designed is structurally sound.

Half the time, my designs were rejected and I had to learn how to let it go. I just recycled the designs for projects that can afford it or for concepts that I find it appropriate.

Philamlife 65 year anniversary and Unilab Awards Night

A tablet holder design for Mall of Asia Arena that was sadly rejected.

I was solving design challenges which kept me interested in this line of work. It felt like I was a product designer and I had to design solutions for the clients while also thinking of the people who will be navigating those spaces.

A modular booth that can fit different spaces and can be transported easily

I think more than the skills I learned in designing, a big part of what I really learned is how to understand the brief, the client, and the intended audience of the design. Would the booth be something that the audience would notice? How would they learn more about the client’s brand if the people visited the space? How can we save on cost? These are just some of the questions I think about when I start a project.

It was a brutal 3 years working late nights and early mornings to finish pitch decks, but I survived, thrived, and enjoyed it.
