What's the BIG IDEA?

Personal and work related notes, thoughts, and ideas
September 15, 2020 · 3 min · 497 words · Lloyd | 

In a pitch or presentation, the big idea is the jump-off point between the problem and the solution conveyed in a few words. It is where all the tagline, visuals, copies, hashtags, media, and other ideas will come from so that everything will be on-strat and on-brand. It is usually written in the following formula:

Consumer Insight + Brand Truth = BIG IDEA#

The big idea will come from how well one understands the target market and the brand. Let’s break it down.

Consumer Insight - This is basically quantitative and qualitative data about the target market. Build up personas based on the consumer data like demographics, interests, transactions, behaviors, and others. Do a survey or interview individuals that best approximate these personas to know what issues they have that can be solved by the brand. If pressed for time, one can extrapolate insights and then verify later on. The output for a consumer insight is usually a profile and an excerpt (from the interview) like the one below.

Brand Truth - Based on the insight gathered, what now can the brand contribute to help the consumers? What are the value propositions that will fit with the insight? To help come up with the brand truth, here’s a simple template:

With [the brand] you can [solution] because [RTBs or reasons to believe]

Using the example above:

With the juice brand, you can meet your daily nutrient intake at the comfort of your own home, because the juice brand offer healthy juice drinks that can keep your immunity up while its nationwide network enables it to deliver within your area! There are also deals available on the website, just subscribe for free and watch out for promo codes!

Don’t worry about making the copy sound great at this point.

The Big Idea - The consumer insight and brand truth now acts as a guide to come up with the Big Idea. This is not yet the strategy, but rather an overall idea that is backed up by data where everything can stem from. Using the example above, here’s the big idea that I came up with.

I wanted to highlight the subscription aspect of the website, that’s why I used harvesting and reaping rewards as an analogy. Subscribing is the seed that can sprout deals for an immunity boosting health drink which can eventually lead to a healthy life.

With that initial keyword, I came up with the tagline: "Reap the rewards of good health". Starting with that, it will be easy to come up with what, when, where, and how to use technology to push a relevant message to the proper audience.
