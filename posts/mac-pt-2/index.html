How I built my own Mac with parts from Lazada and 30k Pesos (pt.2)

Personal and work related notes, thoughts, and ideas
August 31, 2020 · 2 min · 308 words · Lloyd | 

Part 1 focused on the Hardware, the next part of the build is the software or the operating system. Before starting this, you need to have a mac where you can download the latest and official OS via the App Store and a spare 8GB usb flash drive. During the time that I completed my build, the latest was Mac OS Mojave.

Here are some of the other pre-requisites from the guide that I followed:

  1. An 8+GB USB flash drive
  2. The Install OS X/macOS.app (preferably downloaded direct from the app store)
  3. Clover’s Install Package (courtesy of Dids)
  4. Clover Configurator (the brave can edit with any text editor - but CC is typically quicker)

Make sure you get the Global edition

  1. VirtualSMC.kext - this supercedes FakeSMC.kext as our SMC emulator and either VirtualSMC or FakeSMC is vital to booting our Hackintosh. Without one of them, we’d never boot.
  2. Any other kexts (Kernel Extensions) for our mobo/etc

Just follow the guide and by the end of it, you should have an installer. Plug that USB into the Hackintosh you’re building and boot it up to install the Mac OS.

This was actually easier than I expected. Wifi and bluetooth works out of the box. I could even use the Messages app and send SMS from my Mac! Even Airdrop sending and receiving works!

My only problem is that it sometimes freezes, specially when I turn off the monitor and leave it on idle for a long time. If that happens, I have to reboot. To prevent this from happening, I leave my monitor with screensaver turned on.

I use my Hackintosh as my daily driver until today, but I haven’t updated to the latest OS (Mac OS Catalina) yet as I’m afraid that it might break compatibility with my hardware.
